What is TEDxBerkeley?

On February 6, 2016, TEDxBerkeley is gathering world-thinkers, makers, and doers at UC Berkeley to discuss "Finding X". The audience will be composed of a diverse audience of over 2000 students, professors, and thought leaders from Berkeley and beyond for a stimulating day of presentations, discussions, entertainment and art that will spark new ideas and opportunities for all.

FAQ

  • Where is the event?

    TEDxBerkeley is hosted in Zellerbach Hall on the UC Berkeley Campus, you can find a map to Zellerbach Hall here: http://bit.ly/1dwaXfS.

  • When should I arrive?

    Registration will be from 8:30 to 9:30AM at Zellerbach Hall. We expect our event to be sold out this year with over 1800 attendees, so come early for registration. Please bring a printed copy of your ticket and ID with you. Students must bring current student ID. If you would like to participate in the wine reception to follow Session 3, you must bring ID.

  • How should I arrive?

    Parking can be difficult in Berkeley, we recommend that you use public transportation to get to Berkeley. You can plan a trip to arrive at the Downtown Berkeley Bart station, which is a short walk to Zellerbach Hall, on http://www.bart.gov/. If you are driving to Berkeley, you can a description of your parking options here: http://bit.ly/1bEhGYI.

  • Will there be lunch?

    If you purchased a lunch along with your ticket then you will be able to pick up your boxed lunch in Pauley Ballroom at the lunch break. Otherwise, you have the opportunity to explore Berkeleyâ€™s local restaurants. A map including some local restaurants will be included in your program.

  • When does the event end?

    Sessions will end at 5:15 PM, although we encourage you to attend our wine reception until 6:30 PM in Pauley Ballroom.
